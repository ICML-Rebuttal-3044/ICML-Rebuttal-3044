Out-of-Distribution Observation Generation

Figure 1

Preliminary qualitative results under object-pose and lighting variations. We show the cases to clarify whether ViMoGen can extend beyond the primary setting studied in the main paper. Top: the target object is rotated by 90° (object pose variation). Bottom: additional illumination is introduced (lighting variation).

In both cases, ViMoGen is able to generate task-relevant and reasonably coherent observations, although the visual quality remains imperfect. These examples provide preliminary evidence that ViMoGen can generalize beyond the default setting, while indicating that robustness under broader pose and appearance changes still deserves further study. Exploring stronger generalization under such variations is an important direction for future work.

Real-World Experiments

Figure 2

Preliminary real-robot qualitative results of ViMoGen.

Figure: Preliminary real-robot qualitative results of ViMoGen.

We show two representative real-robot cases to clarify whether ViMoGen can also apply beyond simulation. Top: a pick-and-place example, where the robot picks up the yellow cube and places it into the red bowl. Bottom: a stacking example, where the robot picks up the yellow cube and places it on top of the purple cube.

In both cases, ViMoGen is able to generate task-relevant and reasonably coherent observations on real-robot scenes, although the visual quality is not perfect. These examples provide preliminary evidence that our method can extend to real-robot settings and produce usable observations beyond the simulated setup.
